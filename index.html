Democratizing Governance of Government Schools

Our Just Cause: Empower people to have voice and agency to represent their interests and participate in the governance of their local public institutions.

THE CRISIS

Government schools in India are governed poorly (52% schools’ governing body(SMC) meets less than 9 times in a year).This increases the probability of schools not being able to deliver on their intended outcomes.

With only 13.2% of government schools in the country comply with the 10 basic parameters laid down in the Right To Education.This means that children studying in a whooping 87% government schools in the country are not being given basic facilities such as electricity, clean drinking water, good teachers.1 in 3 schools in India does not have an electricity connection

This is to say nothing of parameters such as benches in classroom, legible blackboard which are not even tracked.These factors contribute to poor learning for children with only 50% Grade 5 children are able to read grade 2 text.




Mission, Vision, Our Theory Of Change

Mission

Fostering highly functional inclusive local platforms meant for local collective dialogue and action. We activate/ strengthen these platforms by fostering voice and agency in local stakeholders to participate in such platforms.

Vision

Building a culture of shared ownership around government schools.



Our Theory Of Change

A well governed government school is more likely to deliver on its intended outcomes than a poorly governed school. If SMCs function well, a government school is likely to be governed better than if only the principal governs the school.

↓

Inclusion and representation of all stakeholders are basic facets of good governance. SMCs are mandatorily composed of a diverse stakeholder set including parents, teachers, students, principal, local authority representative etc.

↓

To govern effectively, those governing should have the requisite voice and agency. In case of a government school, the SMC is the governing body.

↓

Samarthya fosters voice and agency in members of the School Management Committee which is a legally mandated governance platform meant for local collective dialogue and action.





Our Approach

In the context of public education, we activate School Management Committees. Samarthya fosters voice and agency in local stakeholders enabling them to dialogue and take collective action. The local stakeholders include the parent, teacher, student and local authority representatives. We conduct the following activities to achieve this:

  1. Build capacity in citizens to engage effectively as SMC members and with SMCs. This is either done by interactive training sessions or by modeling inside SMC meetings.
  2. Support SMC members in recognizing and building alignment on issues they want to solve inside a school. They do this by facilitating sessions outside the school or by facilitating SMC meetings.
  3. Help calibrate the process of election of SMCs to have a greater representation from the local community and to have free and fair elections for the SMC members.

      4. Through the aforementioned interventions, we enable SMC members to brainstorm and problem solve, together and locally.
